Learning

Growing up, I had an interest in computer technologies. When I was young, I believe my uncle had this waterfall java applet(not 100% sure) display on an HTML page - copied from another page. In my teenage years, there was an interest in internet marketing, which is related to websites and how they are structured; even though I did not understand much of it back then. Now, I am on the path of learning through online resources, specifically through: App Academy Open. App Academy is a pretty prestigious software engineering bootcamp with physical locations both in California and New York. They offer a curriculum to students in an online or in-person setting, to which they go through an entire curriculum covering web development topics and practices.

This specialized training comes at a price for the mentorship and resources. However, 'App Academy Open', is the 'open-source' version of their priced bootcamps. The curriculum is associated with that of the online bootcamp offered. This course does not come with mentorship, or resources related to job search guidance that the paid bootcamp maintains. Although, when picking between the options available, the free version may seem like the easy choice to make, however, in my opinion, it does take a significant amount of effort, depending on the person, to be able to complete the free version of the bootcamp without the help of App Academy staff. If you are thinking about a career in software engineering, I would advice undertaking this curriculum, as they recently released an updated version in April 2022. While moving through the curriculum, you will be able to see the quality of the material and if you feel that finishing the modules without help is too diffult but you would still want to forego the content and have the money, then consider taking the bootcamp; in-person or online.
